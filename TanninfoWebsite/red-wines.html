Red Wines

It's been said that drinking red wine, in moderation, is actually good for you. Have you ever wondered why? It's an interesting concept, especially since you wouldn't typically think an alcoholic beverage to be GOOD for you, but in this case, it is. Red wines contain certain antioxidants that may actually help prevent the disease that typically causes heart attacks. The skin from the grapes used to make red wines contain resveratrol which may be what helps prevent damage to any blood vessels and prevents blood clots.

Now which red wines are best for you? That is the whole premise behind this website. Trying to find you the best wines for you, and the best wines in your area. There are dozens of wine variations ranging from crisp and light to robust and sweet. It isn't easy figuring out what's for you if this is really your first time making this decision so that's why we put together a database to help determine the top selling wines in the surrounding areas of Atlanta, as well as a flavor profile and food pairings page. Our flavor profile and food pairings page will better help you understand the best wines for you as far as taste, and helps you decide the best foods to pair with your new found wines.

Cabernet Sauvignon

By far our top suggestion. The Cabernet Sauvignon is our most popular wine amongst the wineries in Atlanta. The Cabernet Sauvignon is best known for it's darkcolor and full body. This wine is dry and has a healthy level of tannin. This is best paired with a steak.

Cep Pinot Noir

Our second best wine suggestion. The Pinot Noir is our second most popular wine amongst the wineries in Atlanta. With a very complex flavor profile, this wine pairs well with any mushroom, salmon, or porkchop dish.

Chateau du Champ des Treilles

A savory and classic wine, the Chateau du Champ des Treilles best pairs with red meats and venison.

Le Combal Cahors

Producing an extraordinarily deep dark red, almost black, color. The Le Combal Cahors best pairs with Duck, mushrooms, and even chocolate.
